In this study we are interested in assessing how people manage their E-mails. Nowadays, people are often overwhelmed with large number of E-mail communications and the management of these E-mails can become a difficult day-to-day task. We're interested in assessing people's E-mail response decisions and the factors that influence their decisions.

In this task, you will be presented with a number of prototype E-mails, both personal and work related, taken from the inbox of many potential E-mail users. Your goal is to examine each E-mail, with the aim of assisting in their correct classification as: “Phishing” or “Ham”. You will also be asked to determine your confidence in this classification, and the action that you would take with each E-mail.

A “Phishing” E-mail is a deceptive E-mail pretending to be from a legitimate source (e.g., bank, friend) to steal money or information. A “Ham” E-mail is a legitimate email. For each successful E-mail classification, you will be rewarded with 1 point. Each point you earn will translate into a monetary incentive as advertised.

The figure below shows an example of the interface that you will use in every round. During each round you will answer two questions:
(1) Is this a phishing email?
(2) How confident are you for your answer in question 1?
Then submit your responses by clicking on the "Done" button.


This study involves three phases. During Phase 1 and 3, you will NOT get any feedback whether you classify the E-mails correctly or incorrectly. You will only receive a summary of the points earned at the end. However, in Phase 2, you will receive feedback if you incorrectly classify a phishing email as ham.
